**Multi-Omic Integration and Cellular Communication in Cardiovascular Pathology and Genetic Variation: A Comprehensive Literature Review**

**多组学整合与心血管病理及遗传变异中的细胞通讯：综合文献综述**

---

### Introduction / 引言

The rapid evolution of single-cell technologies and proteomic profiling has revolutionized our understanding of complex biological systems, particularly in cardiovascular diseases and genetic regulation. Modern research increasingly focuses on deciphering cell-cell communications and the functional impact of genetic variants at a granular level. From the mechanical sculpting of embryos [13] to the metabolic orchestration of the endothelium [5], the integration of multi-omic data provides a roadmap for precision medicine. This review synthesizes recent advancements in analytical frameworks, cardiovascular pathology (specifically aortic and cardiac conditions), and the computational classification of genetic mutations.

单细胞技术和蛋白质组学分析的飞速发展彻底改变了我们对复杂生物系统（尤其是心血管疾病和遗传调控）的理解。现代研究日益侧重于在细胞层面解码细胞间通讯以及遗传变异的功能影响。从胚胎发育的机械塑造 [13] 到内皮细胞的代谢编排 [5]，多组学数据的整合为精准医学提供了路线图。本综述综合了分析框架、心血管病理（特别是主动脉和心脏疾病）以及遗传突变计算分类方面的最新进展。

---

### 1. Advanced Analytical Frameworks for Single-Cell and Proteomic Profiling
### 1. 单细胞与蛋白质组学分析的高级框架

Current methodologies strive to overcome the inherent noise and sparsity of single-cell data. The GIRAFI framework [2] has been developed to recover biological structures in sparse single-cell proteomics, while spectral prompting techniques [18] allow for the recovery of cell-type architectures from transcriptomic datasets. The integration of Artificial Intelligence is exemplified by OmniCellAgent [6], an AI scientist for omic-driven discovery, and ChatDIA [16], which utilizes large language models for targeted analysis of mass spectrometry data. Furthermore, tools like SimSpace [17] provide simulation frameworks for spatial omics, and new estimators for unique molecular identifiers (UMIs) improve the accuracy of RNA-seq quantification [33]. These innovations are supported by objective peak detection in LC-MS/MS [35] and supervised repeat annotation using protein embeddings [34], ensuring high-fidelity data interpretation.

目前的算法致力于克服单细胞数据固有的噪声和稀疏性。GIRAFI 框架 [2] 被开发用于恢复稀疏单细胞蛋白质组中的生物结构，而光谱提示技术 [18] 则允许从转录组数据集中恢复细胞类型架构。人工智能的整合以 OmniCellAgent [6]（用于组学驱动发现的 AI 科学家）和 ChatDIA [16]（利用大语言模型进行质谱数据定向分析）为代表。此外，SimSpace [17] 等工具为空间组学提供了模拟框架，而唯一分子标识符 (UMI) 的新估计方法提高了 RNA-seq 定量的准确性 [33]。这些创新得到了 LC-MS/MS 中客观峰检测 [35] 和使用蛋白质嵌入的监督重复注释 [34] 的支持，确保了高保真度的数据解读。

### 2. Cellular Heterogeneity and Communication in Aortic and Cardiac Pathologies
### 2. 主动脉与心脏病理中的细胞异质性与通讯

Research into vascular and cardiac health highlights the critical role of cellular interactions. In the context of the aorta, delivery of mitochondria has been shown to attenuate elastase-induced abdominal aortic aneurysms [1], suggesting a therapeutic pathway via metabolic restoration. In atherosclerotic plaques, the deletion of PKM2 in macrophages promotes stabilization but not regression [19], emphasizing the context-dependent nature of myeloid cells. Cardiac fibrosis is another area of intense focus; Leptin Receptor-positive (LepR+) fibroblasts have been identified as preferential contributors to maladaptive fibrosis [21, 22]. Furthermore, the discovery of a Liver-to-Atria inflammatory axis suggests that interorgan communication significantly drives arrhythmias [24]. These studies collectively underscore how specific cell trajectories, such as those regulated by mineralocorticoid receptors [20, 29], dictate the progression of diastolic dysfunction and heart failure.

心血管健康研究强调了细胞相互作用的关键作用。在主动脉研究中，线粒体输送已被证明能减轻弹性蛋白酶诱导的腹主动脉瘤 [1]，这提示了通过代谢恢复进行治疗的途径。在动脉粥样硬化斑块中，巨噬细胞中 PKM2 的缺失促进了斑块稳定而非消退 [19]，强调了骨髓细胞的背景依赖性。心脏纤维化是另一个研究重点；瘦素受体阳性 (LepR+) 成纤维细胞被认为是适应不良性纤维化的主要贡献者 [21, 22]。此外，肝-房炎症轴的发现表明器官间通讯显著驱动了心律失常 [24]。这些研究共同强调了特定的细胞轨迹（如受盐皮质激素受体调节的轨迹 [20, 29]）如何决定舒张功能障碍和心力衰竭的进展。

### 3. Metabolic Orchestration and Mitochondrial Dynamics
### 3. 代谢编排与线粒体动力学

The endothelium acts as a metabolic conductor, orchestrating communication across various organs [5]. Disruptions in these pathways, such as Endothelial NOX1 signaling, can drive obesity through skeletal muscle mitochondrial dysfunction [7]. In pulmonary hypertension, mitochondrial mitotic fission regulated by Dynamin 2 is a key pathological driver [12]. Metabolomic roadmaps are now being used to define right ventricular function in pulmonary hypertension [10, 11, 32], providing systemic insights into metabolic memory and its role in halting cardiac hypertrophy via PDK4 [31]. The influence of external factors, such as microplastics, also contributes to myocardial fibrosis through complex molecular mechanisms [30].

内皮细胞充当代谢导体，协调不同器官之间的通讯 [5]。这些通路的紊乱（如内皮 NOX1 信号转导）可通过骨骼肌线粒体功能障碍引发肥胖 [7]。在肺动脉高压中，由 Dynamin 2 调节的线粒体有丝分裂裂变是关键的病理驱动因素 [12]。代谢组学路线图现在正被用于定义肺动脉高压中的右心室功能 [10, 11, 32]，为代谢记忆及其通过 PDK4 阻止心脏肥大的作用提供了系统性的见解 [31]。外部因素（如微塑料）的影响也通过复杂的分子机制导致心肌纤维化 [30]。

### 4. Functional Characterization of Genetic Variants and Regulatory Networks
### 4. 遗传变异与调控网络的功能表征

Understanding the functional impact of mutations is essential for diagnosing and treating genetic disorders. CharacTERT, a machine learning tool, has been developed to classify hTERT missense variants [3]. Similarly, phylogenetically estimated fitness effects help determine the impact of mutations in viral proteins like influenza [9]. At the regulatory level, single-cell analysis uncovers transcription factor networks underlying processes like T-cell aging [8] and B-cell development [26]. Epigenetic programs can now be deciphered through network-based predictions of clustered open regulatory elements [25]. Additionally, post-transcriptional regulation is being explored through tools like Ribo-Tweezer, which reveals layers of gene regulation by removing ribosomal proteins [28], and the study of nitric oxide’s role in driving proteomic diversity through alternative splicing [14].

理解突变的功能影响对于诊断和治疗遗传性疾病至关重要。机器学习工具 CharacTERT 已被开发用于对 hTERT 错义变异进行分类 [3]。类似地，系统发育估计的适应性效应有助于确定流感等病毒蛋白突变的影响 [9]。在调控层面，单细胞分析揭示了 T 细胞衰老 [8] 和 B 细胞发育 [26] 等过程背后的转录因子网络。现在可以通过基于网络的集群开放调控元件预测来解码表观遗传程序 [25]。此外，通过 Ribo-Tweezer 等工具正在探索转录后调控，该工具通过移除核糖体蛋白揭示了多层基因调控 [28]，以及研究一氧化氮通过选择性剪接驱动蛋白质组多样性的作用 [14]。

---

### Conclusion / 结论

The convergence of single-cell multi-omics, advanced computational modeling, and functional genomics is providing unprecedented clarity into cardiovascular and genetic pathologies. Challenges remain regarding population representation in single-cell atlases [4] and the need for multimodal retrieval layers for biomedical agents [27]. However, the ability to map spatiotemporal forces [13], identify context-dependent therapeutic mechanisms [23, 25], and classify variants [3] marks a new era in precision medicine. Future research must continue to integrate these diverse data types to move from descriptive profiling to mechanistic intervention.

单细胞多组学、高级计算建模和功能基因组学的融合，为心血管和遗传病理学提供了前所未有的清晰度。单细胞图谱中的群体代表性问题 [4] 以及对生物医学智能体多模态检索层的需求 [27] 仍然是挑战。然而，绘制时空力图谱 [13]、识别背景依赖性治疗机制 [23, 25] 以及对变异进行分类 [3] 的能力，标志着精准医学进入了一个新时代。未来的研究必须继续整合这些不同类型的数据，从描述性分析转向机制性干预。

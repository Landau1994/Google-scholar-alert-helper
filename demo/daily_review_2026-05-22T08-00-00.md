# Advances in Precision Medicine: Integrating Single-Cell Omics, CRISPR Gene Editing, and AI-Driven Structural Biology in Cardiovascular and Aortic Diseases

# 医药精准医学新进展：整合单细胞组学、CRISPR基因编辑与AI驱动的结构生物学在心血管及主动脉疾病中的应用

### Introduction
The landscape of modern biomedical research is being rapidly reshaped by the convergence of high-resolution "omics" technologies, programmable genome editing, and artificial intelligence. This literature review synthesizes recent findings from 228 papers, focusing on how single-cell proteomics and transcriptomics are elucidating cell-cell communications, how CRISPR-based screens and base editors are identifying disease-linked variants, and how AI tools like AlphaFold are accelerating our understanding of protein structures. Special attention is given to vascular pathologies, particularly aortic diseases and hypertension, where smooth muscle and endothelial cell dysfunctions are being decoded at an unprecedented scale.

### 引言
现代生物医学研究的版图正在因高分辨率“组学”技术、可编程基因组编辑和人工智能的融合而迅速重塑。本综述综合了228篇论文的最新研究成果，重点探讨单细胞蛋白质组学和转录组学如何阐明细胞间通讯，CRISPR筛选和碱基编辑器如何鉴定疾病相关变异，以及AlphaFold等人工智能工具如何加速我们对蛋白质结构的理解。本文特别关注血管病变，尤其是主动脉疾病和高血压，在这些领域中，平滑肌和内皮细胞的功能障碍正以史无前例的规模被解码。

---

### 1. Multi-modal Single-Cell Technologies and Cell-Cell Communications
The transition from bulk tissue analysis to single-cell resolution has revealed immense cellular heterogeneity [91, 95]. Advanced computational frameworks like SigFormer and MetaCCI now allow researchers to model transcellular signaling and microenvironment interactions with high interpretability [19, 109]. Single-cell proteomics (SCP) has emerged as a critical practical tool, filling the gap between mRNA levels and functional protein expression [34]. Integration of spatial transcriptomics further maps the immunoregulatory architecture of tissues [4, 49], while multimodal lineage tracing methods like BARseq3 and synthetic barcoding provide a dynamic view of how cell fates are committed over time [9, 23]. These technologies are essential for identifying specific cell-state transitions in complex environments, such as the spatial heterogeneity of the lung or the male breast cancer microenvironment [89, 121].

### 1. 多模态单细胞技术与细胞间通讯
从体组织分析向单细胞分辨率的转变揭示了巨大的细胞异质性 [91, 95]。SigFormer和MetaCCI等先进计算框架现在允许研究人员以高解释性模拟跨细胞信号传导和微环境相互作用 [19, 109]。单细胞蛋白质组学（SCP）已成为关键的实用工具，填补了mRNA水平与功能蛋白表达之间的空白 [34]。空间转录组学的整合进一步绘制了组织的免疫调节架构 [4, 49]，而BARseq3和合成条形码等多模态谱系追踪方法则为细胞命运随时间的演变提供了动态视角 [9, 23]。这些技术对于识别复杂环境中的特定细胞状态转换至关重要，例如肺部的空间异质性或男性乳腺癌的微环境 [89, 121]。

### 2. CRISPR-Based Functional Genomics and Genetic Variants
CRISPR technology has evolved beyond simple gene knocking out to sophisticated base editing and large-scale screening. Machine learning-powered combinatorial mutagenesis is now used to rapidly customize base editors for clinical applications, such as treating β-thalassaemia [3, 5]. CRISPR activation screens have identified pan-orthoflavivirus restriction factors like SPART [1], while others have addressed T cell exhaustion [30, 144]. Crucially, single-cell chromatin accessibility data is being used to connect polygenic disease risk variants to specific cell states and regulatory programs [26]. For instance, coronary artery disease risk variants in endothelial regulatory elements have been shown to modulate Notch signaling [11]. The development of "genome writing" tools like INSTALL allows for large DNA integration by bypassing innate immunity, further expanding the potential for therapeutic intervention [48].

### 2. 基于CRISPR的功能基因组学与遗传变异
CRISPR技术已从简单的基因敲除进化到复杂的碱基编辑和大规模筛选。机器学习驱动的组合诱变现在被用于快速定制碱基编辑器，以用于临床应用，如治疗β-地中海贫血 [3, 5]。CRISPR激活筛选已鉴定出如SPART等泛正黄病毒限制因子 [1]，而其他筛选则针对T细胞耗竭进行了研究 [30, 144]。至关重要的是，单细胞染色质可及性数据正被用于将多基因疾病风险变异与特定细胞状态和调节程序联系起来 [26]。例如，内皮调节元件中的冠心病风险变异已被证明可以调节Notch信号通路 [11]。INSTALL等“基因组书写”工具的发展允许通过规避先天免疫实现大片段DNA整合，进一步扩大了治疗干预的潜力 [48]。

### 3. Molecular Mechanisms of Aortic and Arterial Diseases
Research into aortic diseases highlights the pivotal role of vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs) and endothelial cells (ECs). In abdominal aortic aneurysms (AAA), the up-regulation of TRPM8 channels attenuates VSMC dysfunction [37]. In the context of calcific aortic valve disease (CAVD), ILK-dependent modulation of DPP4 has been identified as a preventive mechanism [7]. Hypertension research has shifted toward understanding endothelial homeostasis, with factors like MYDGF and SVEP1 playing regulatory roles [98, 99]. Furthermore, lactylation and other post-translational modifications are being recognized as key drivers of atherosclerosis progression [50, 112]. Innovative strategies targeting perivascular adipose tissue and using iPSC-derived ECs for conduit implantation showcase the translational potential of these mechanistic insights [142, 176].

### 3. 主动脉及动脉疾病的分子机制
针对主动脉疾病的研究强调了血管平滑肌细胞（VSMCs）和内皮细胞（ECs）的关键作用。在腹主动脉瘤（AAA）中，TRPM8通道的上调减轻了VSMC功能障碍 [37]。在钙化性主动脉瓣疾病（CAVD）背景下，ILK依赖的DPP4调节被确定为一种预防机制 [7]。高血压研究已转向理解内皮稳态，MYDGF和SVEP1等因子发挥着调节作用 [98, 99]。此外，乳酸化和其他翻译后修饰正被认为是动脉粥样硬化进展的关键驱动因素 [50, 112]。针对血管周围脂肪组织的创新策略以及使用iPSC衍生的内皮细胞进行导管植入，展示了这些机制见解的转化潜力 [142, 176]。

---

### 4. AI, AlphaFold, and Structural Biology in Drug Discovery
Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing structural biology and drug design. AlphaFold 3, while revolutionary, faces challenges in predicting binding poses in heterochiral complexes, indicating areas for improvement [22, 85]. Beyond protein folding, deep learning models like SpecGP and SigFormer predict glycopeptide spectra and interpret transcellular signaling [10, 19]. Foundation models for single-cell data are being benchmarked for their ability to reconstruct gene regulatory networks (GRNs) [14, 31]. In the clinical space, AI-HeartAge models and proteomic aging clocks are being validated for risk stratification in cardiovascular medicine [57, 72]. Large language models (LLMs) are also being optimized to generate functional gene embeddings, providing new blueprints for understanding condition-dependent gene architectures [88, 226].

### 4. AI、AlphaFold 与结构生物学在药物研发中的应用
人工智能正在彻底改变结构生物学和药物设计。AlphaFold 3虽然具有革命性，但在预测异手性复合物的结合姿态方面仍面临挑战，这指出了改进的方向 [22, 85]。除蛋白质折叠外，SpecGP和SigFormer等深度学习模型可预测糖肽光谱并解释跨细胞信号传导 [10, 19]。单细胞数据的基础模型正针对其重建基因调节网络（GRNs）的能力进行基准测试 [14, 31]。在临床领域，AI-HeartAge模型和蛋白质组学衰老时钟正被验证用于心血管医学的风险分层 [57, 72]。大语言模型（LLMs）也正在被优化以生成功能性基因嵌入，为理解条件依赖性基因架构提供了新蓝图 [88, 226]。

---

### 5. Aging, Preeclampsia, and Maternal-Fetal Health
The study of senescence and aging-structured cell populations has revealed that damage-driven irreversibility is a hallmark of biological aging [151, 152]. These processes are particularly relevant in preeclampsia, where placental senescence and endothelial dysfunction impact both maternal and fetal cardiovascular health [61, 119, 165]. Recent studies have utilized proteomic risk scores and metabolomic foundation models to predict kidney disease progression and mirror tissue senescence in biofluids [63, 150, 200]. Understanding how dietary factors like sodium or soy modulate the microbiome and immune tolerance further integrates lifestyle factors into the precision medicine framework [98, 143, 172].

### 5. 衰老、先兆子痫与母婴健康
对衰老及衰老结构细胞群的研究揭示了损伤驱动的不可逆性是生物衰老的标志 [151, 152]。这些过程在先兆子痫中尤为重要，胎盘衰老和内皮功能障碍会影响母体和胎儿的心血管健康 [61, 119, 165]。最近的研究利用蛋白质组风险评分和代谢组基础模型来预测肾脏疾病进展，并在生物体液中反映组织衰老 [63, 150, 200]。了解钠或大豆等饮食因素如何调节微生物组和免疫耐受，进一步将生活方式因素整合到了精准医学框架中 [98, 143, 172]。

---

### Conclusion
The integration of single-cell multi-omics, CRISPR-based functional genomics, and AI-driven structural analysis is providing a holistic view of human disease. From decoding the complex cell-cell interactions in aortic walls to predicting drug responses through genetic variants, these advancements pave the way for highly personalized therapeutic strategies. Future research must continue to bridge the gap between high-throughput data generation and causal biological explanations to fully realize the potential of precision medicine.

### 结论
单细胞多模态组学、基于CRISPR的功能基因组学与AI驱动的结构分析的整合，正在提供人类疾病的全局视角。从解码主动脉壁复杂的细胞间通讯，到通过遗传变异预测药物反应，这些进展为高度个性化的治疗策略奠定了基础。未来的研究必须继续弥合高通量数据生成与因果生物学解释之间的鸿沟，以充分实现精准医学的潜力。
